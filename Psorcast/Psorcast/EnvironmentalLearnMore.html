If you’re reading this, then you’d like to know how we’re keeping your location data private. So let’s dig in to the details.

When we access your phone’s GPS, we keep the latitude and longitude data locally on the device, not on our servers. Then we use your location to access weather and air quality information where you are (we use AirNow and OpenWeather).

We only send that environmental information to our servers for the research study. But we get rid of the latitude and longitude data, because it’s safer all around if we never see that information, let alone store it.

With all of that being said, we can’t guarantee your privacy, so only agree to location services and HealthKit if you’re comfortable doing so.
